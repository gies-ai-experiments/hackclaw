# Frequently Asked Questions

## General

**Q: What is the Gies AI for Impact Challenge?**
A: A 24-hour hackathon where Gies students build AI agents that automate real business workflows using no-code/low-code tools. It runs from 5 PM Thursday April 23 to 5 PM Friday April 24, 2026.

**Q: Who can participate?**
A: Any Gies College of Business undergraduate or graduate student.

**Q: Do I need coding experience?**
A: No. This is a no-code/low-code event. The focus is on building AI agents using tools like Microsoft Copilot Studio and other agent builders.

**Q: Do I need a team?**
A: Yes, teams of 2–4 members. You can come with a team or form one during the networking session (5:00–5:45 PM, Day 1).

**Q: What if I don't have a team yet?**
A: Come to the networking and team formation session at 5:00 PM on Day 1. We'll help you find teammates.

## Rules & Submissions

**Q: What can I build?**
A: An AI agent that automates a multi-step business process. See the tracks: Finance, HR, Marketing, Business Technology, or General Innovation.

**Q: What can I NOT build?**
A: Simple chatbots, single-prompt solutions, or anything that isn't real process automation.

**Q: When are submissions due?**
A: **12:00 PM**, Friday April 24 — hard cutoff. Judging starts at 1:00 PM. Between 12:00 and 1:00 PM hackclaw emails every team their designated judging room, so watch your inbox during lunch.

**Q: What do I need for my submission?**
A: A short slide deck (intro + workflow) and a working live demo.

**Q: How long is my presentation?**
A: Round 1: 7 minutes total (1 min setup, 3 min slides+demo, 2 min Q&A, 1 min transition). Finals: 5 min presentation + 3 min Q&A.

## Getting Help

**Q: How do I get help during the hackathon?**
A: Find a technical mentor in your hacking room, ask in the Discord, or attend champion office hours (9–11 AM Friday).

**Q: What are champion office hours?**
A: Friday 9–11 AM — project champions (Gies staff from each track) are available for domain-specific guidance and user testing.

**Q: Where is the workshop info?**
A: Visit https://build-a-thon.dsrs.illinois.edu/ for student workshop details.

## Judging

**Q: How does judging work?**
A: Two rounds. Round 1: you present in your track (parallel sessions). Top 1 team per track advances. Round 2: 5 finalists present to a combined panel. Winners announced at 4 PM.

**Q: Will I get feedback?**
A: Yes. Judges use a rubric and feedback will be shared with all teams after the event.

**Q: What are judges looking for?**
A: Problem significance, automation depth (not just one step), working demo, business impact, innovation, and presentation quality.
